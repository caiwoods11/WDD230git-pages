Papa Jay's Celebrates 25 Years

Papa Jay’s celebrated 25 years of business on March 12 and like they have every year they invited the public to join them.

In 1996, Jay Moyle restored the only store in Clifton to sell Papa Jay’s Gourmet Hickory Smoked Beef. Something he spent a great deal of time perfecting before taking that step. The available flavors have expanded since then offering something for just about anyone who enjoys jerky. An icebox that was in that original store one hundred years ago has recently found its way back just in time for the celebration.

Mary Penrod, currently runs the business with her husband Richard and about 15 employees. She said it was the best turnout to date with over 150 customers dropping in throughout the day. They offered a “wheelchair of fortune” to spin for prizes made from a wheelchair wheel. In honor of the 25 years they have a 25 cent prize on the wheel that they swapped out for $25 a few times during the day.

In addition to spinning the wheel, Papa Jay’s had door prizes and some nice sales going on. Every bit of their famous roast beef sandwiches were gone by the end of the day.

Written By: Teresa Chipman
